## 2024-05-18 - Avoid np.polyfit for small histograms
**Learning:** `np.polyfit` relies on full generalized SVD operations under the hood (often via LAPACK), adding large fixed overhead. When performing 1D regression on very small arrays, such as histogram bin contents in `.to_hist().values()`, this overhead dominates processing time, leading to significant slow-downs when applied iteratively.
**Action:** When a simple 1D linear OLS regression is needed (e.g. $y = mx + c$), replace `np.polyfit(x, y, 1)` with a manual calculation using vectorized `np.sum()`. This change alone can yield a ~3x performance speedup on small array sizes. Use `np.errstate(divide='ignore', invalid='ignore')` safely around divisions on zeroed bins.
